People

A team of blind creators, audio producers, engineers, and mentors.

Core team

Head Designer

Calvin Van Zytveld

Calvin is a Stanford musicology PhD student, composer-cellist, and audio engineer whose research centers the history and present state of audio engineering. After losing his vision during graduate study, he began reimagining audio tools from the standpoint of touch, listening, and nonvisual musical practice.

Director of Operations

Lemon Guo

Lemon is an interdisciplinary artist, composer, and vocalist whose sound work has appeared internationally across museums, radio, and immersive documentary. She brings Blind Envy a producer's sense of form, logistics, and sonic atmosphere.

Head Engineer

Celeste Betancur

Celeste is a Stanford researcher in computer-based music theory and acoustics at CCRMA. She leads technical decisions for the devices and helps translate audio research into working hardware.

Go-to-Market Lead

Bhavya Shah

Bhavya is a Stanford computer science student and blind technologist working across software, accessibility, and product strategy. He helps connect the tools to communities and distribution paths beyond the lab.

Podcast Producer

Elyse Blennerhassett

Elyse is an audio, film, and immersive media producer with roots in oral history and sound art. Her work has appeared with outlets and institutions including BBC World Service, Sundance, and The Invisible Institute.

Podcast Instructor

Ariana Martinez

Ariana is a multimedia artist, audio producer, and media educator. Their work spans BBC Radio, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, and the Disabled Journalists Association.

Production Engineer

Mike Mulshine

Mike is a composer, performer, and Stanford CCRMA PhD student whose work explores accessible and empowering ways to experience and co-create music. His practice bridges instrument-building, sound installation, and performance.

Faculty Advisors

Faculty Advisor

Chris Chafe

Chris is the Duca Family Professor and chair of Stanford Music, and a longtime leader at CCRMA. A composer-cellist and computer-music researcher, he advises Blind Envy on design and production.

Faculty Advisor

Jonathan Berger

Jonathan is the Denning Family Provostial Professor in Music and director of CCRMA at Stanford. His work spans composition, music cognition, and music-technology research.

Faculty Advisor

Sile O'Modhrain

Sile is a University of Michigan professor whose research connects haptics, music performance, and new interfaces. She also worked as a BBC Radio audio engineer and producer.

Faculty Advisor

Seth Cluett

Seth is an artist, composer, scholar, and director of Columbia's Computer Music Center. His work moves through sound installation, perception, and experimental audio technologies.
